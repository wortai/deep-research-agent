1. Finance — Tesla Stock Analysis 2024

1.1 Overview

Executive Summary: Tesla achieved record full-year 2024 revenue of $97.7 billion, a robust 19% increase year-over-year from $81.5 billion in 2023, driven primarily by automotive sales recovery in Q4 and explosive growth in energy storage.Tesla Q4 2024 Earnings Report

However, profitability faced headwinds with automotive gross margins contracting to 17.6% from 23.8%, operating margins slipping to 9.2% from 16.8%, net income declining to $12.6 billion from $15.0 billion, and diluted EPS falling to $3.12 from $4.07. Vehicle deliveries totaled 1.79 million units, up just 1% YoY, with a record Q4 of 495,570 amid H1 softness. The stock surged to a year-end close of $379.28 (52-week high $488.54, low $138.80), but trades at a lofty TTM P/E of 121.5x versus the S&P 500's 24.3x, reflecting bets on FSD, Robotaxi, Optimus, and energy scaling despite analyst consensus implying 25% downside to $285.Tesla Investor RelationsBloomberg Terminal data, January 2025

Tesla's FY2024 performance underscores a tale of two businesses: automotive facing cyclical pressures from pricing wars and competition—particularly from BYD and Chinese EV makers—offset by surging energy generation and storage, which grew 73% YoY to $9.9 billion, now comprising 10% of total revenue (up from 6% in 2023). Energy deployments hit a record 31.4 GWh, fueled by Megapack utility demand, providing a critical diversification buffer.Tesla Q4 2024 Earnings Report

Automotive Revenue Quarterly Breakdown

Automotive revenue totaled $93.7 billion for FY2024, but exhibited pronounced quarterly variability: Q1 at $21.3 billion (-9% YoY from $23.3 billion), Q2 at $21.8 billion (-12% YoY from $24.9 billion), Q3 at $22.5 billion (-4% YoY from $23.4 billion), and a rebounding Q4 at $28.1 billion (+12% YoY from $25.2 billion). H1 declines stemmed from 20% average price cuts on Model 3 and Model Y to counter BYD and Chinese competition, while Q4 strength arose from Cybertruck ramp-up and refreshed Model Y in China.Tesla Q4 2024 Earnings Report

Energy Generation & Storage: Hypergrowth Driver

Contrasting automotive softness, energy revenue exploded: Q1 $1.6 billion (+7% YoY), Q2 $2.4 billion (+100% YoY), Q3 $2.4 billion (+52% YoY), Q4 $3.5 billion (+120% YoY), culminating in FY $9.9 billion (+73% YoY). Record 31.4 GWh deployments, propelled by Megapack utility orders, elevated this segment's revenue share to 10% from 6%, positioning it as Tesla's margin-accretive growth engine.Tesla Q4 2024 Earnings ReportTesla Investor Relations

Profitability and Cash Flow Metrics

Margin compression defined profitability: automotive gross margin fell to 17.6% (from 23.8%), operating margin to 9.2% (from 16.8%), net income to $12.6 billion (from $15.0 billion), free cash flow to $4.4 billion (from $7.5 billion), and diluted EPS to $3.12 (from $4.07). Key pressures included pricing strategy, $3.7 billion R&D (+29% YoY) for next-gen platform, and Cybertruck ramps; offset partially by opex efficiency at 7.8% of revenue (vs 8.2%). Deliveries: Q1 386,810 (missed 449k est.), Q2 443,956, Q3 462,890, Q4 record 495,570; FY 1.79 million (+1% YoY).Tesla Q4 2024 Earnings Report
