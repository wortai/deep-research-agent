# News & Journalism Research Skill

## Identity & Scope
This skill governs research focused on **current events, breaking news, recent developments, and journalistic reporting**. It prioritizes recency, source legitimacy, and factual accuracy. Activates when users ask about things that are happening NOW, recent events, trending topics, or anything requiring the most up-to-date information.

---

## Step 1 — News Category Detection
Determine what type of news coverage is needed:

1. **Breaking / Developing** — Events happening right now or within the last 48 hours
2. **Analysis / Deep Reporting** — Longer investigative pieces explaining why something happened
3. **Industry News** — Sector-specific developments (tech, healthcare, energy, etc.)
4. **Political / Policy** — Government actions, elections, legislation, geopolitical events
5. **Business / Corporate** — Company announcements, leadership changes, product launches
6. **Science / Technology** — Research breakthroughs, product releases, tech industry moves
7. **Sports / Entertainment** — Events, results, cultural moments
8. **Crisis / Disaster** — Natural disasters, security events, public health emergencies

---

## Step 2 — Source Prioritization
News research demands verified, reputable, multi-source coverage:

### Tier 1 — Wire Services & Major Papers
- Associated Press (AP), Reuters, AFP (wire services — factual baseline)
- New York Times, Washington Post, The Guardian, Financial Times, Wall Street Journal

### Tier 2 — Broadcast & Digital
- BBC News, CNN, NPR, PBS NewsHour, Al Jazeera English
- CNBC, Bloomberg (for business/financial news)

### Tier 3 — Specialized / Regional
- TechCrunch, Ars Technica, The Verge (technology)
- Politico, The Hill (US politics)
- Nature, Science Magazine (science)
- ESPN, The Athletic (sports)

### Tier 4 — Alternative & Independent
- ProPublica, The Intercept (investigative)
- Substack journalists with verified track records
- Official government press releases and statements

**Always cross-reference at least 2-3 sources for any factual claim.**

---

## Step 3 — Query Design Through Journalism Lens
Queries must be crafted to surface the LATEST and MOST VERIFIED information:

### Recency Layer
- **What happened?** — Factual timeline of events, who/what/when/where
- **Latest updates** — Most recent developments, statements, official responses
- **Timeline construction** — Chronological sequence of events with timestamps

### Context Layer
- **Why it matters** — Historical context, precedent, significance
- **Stakeholder reactions** — Official statements, expert opinions, public response
- **Related developments** — Connected events, parallel situations, pattern recognition

### Depth Layer
- **Investigation** — Underlying causes, systemic issues, accountability
- **Impact analysis** — Who is affected? What are the consequences?
- **Future trajectory** — What happens next? Expert predictions, scheduled events

---

## Step 4 — Data Representation
- **Lead with the most recent information** — newest first, then context
- **Include specific dates and times** for all events
- **Attribute every claim** to a specific source (outlet name + reporter when possible)
- **Distinguish between**: confirmed facts, official statements, eyewitness accounts, analysis/opinion
- **Flag conflicting reports** clearly — "Source A reports X, while Source B reports Y"
- **Include exact quotes** from key figures when available

---

## Step 5 — Depth Calibration
| User Signal | Response |
|-------------|----------|
| "what happened" / "news about" | Factual summary from multiple sources |
| "latest" / "today" / "breaking" | Most recent developments, prioritize recency |
| "why" / "explain" / "context" | Deep background with historical parallels |
| "reaction" / "response" | Stakeholder statements and expert analysis |
| "impact" / "consequence" | Analysis of short-term and long-term effects |

---

## Anti-Patterns
- ✗ Do NOT treat all sources as equally reliable — wire services > opinion blogs
- ✗ Do NOT present single-source claims as confirmed facts
- ✗ Do NOT ignore the timestamp — a report from 3 days ago may be outdated
- ✗ Do NOT mix opinion/editorial content with factual reporting without labeling
- ✗ Do NOT ignore competing narratives — present what multiple credible sources report
- ✗ Do NOT use social media posts as primary sources unless from verified official accounts
