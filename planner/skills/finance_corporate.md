# Finance & Corporate Research Skill

## Identity & Scope
This skill governs research in **financial markets, corporate analysis, investment research, and economic data**. It requires the precision and rigor expected at Bloomberg, BlackRock, Citadel, Goldman Sachs, or any institutional research desk. Activates for stock analysis, market research, company valuations, macroeconomic analysis, personal finance strategy, fintech, and regulatory topics.

---

## Step 1 — Financial Domain Detection
Finance is multi-dimensional. Identify which domain(s) apply:

1. **Equity Research** — Individual stock analysis, earnings, valuations, price targets
2. **Fixed Income** — Bonds, yields, credit ratings, duration, interest rate sensitivity
3. **Macro Economics** — GDP, inflation, monetary policy, fiscal policy, central bank actions
4. **Corporate Finance** — M&A, IPOs, capital structure, financial statements, cash flow analysis
5. **Personal Finance** — Investing strategy, retirement planning, tax optimization, budgeting
6. **Fintech / Crypto** — Digital assets, blockchain, payment systems, DeFi
7. **Risk Management** — VaR, hedging, derivatives, portfolio risk metrics
8. **Real Estate** — REITs, property valuation, mortgage markets, commercial real estate
9. **Commodities** — Oil, gold, agriculture, metals, supply-demand dynamics
10. **Private Equity / Venture Capital** — Fund structures, deal flow, valuations, exit strategies

Then determine the **analytical perspective**: Is this a trade thesis, a fundamental analysis, a macro overview, or an educational explainer?

---

## Step 2 — Source Prioritization
Financial research demands accurate, timely, and authoritative data:
- **SEC Filings**: 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, proxy statements, insider transactions (EDGAR)
- **Financial Data**: Bloomberg Terminal data, Yahoo Finance, Google Finance, FactSet, S&P Capital IQ
- **Research Reports**: Sell-side analyst reports (Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan, Goldman), buy-side research
- **News Sources**: Reuters, Bloomberg News, Financial Times, Wall Street Journal, CNBC
- **Central Banks**: Federal Reserve (FRED), ECB, Bank of England publications
- **Regulatory Bodies**: SEC, FINRA, CFTC publications
- **Economic Data**: Bureau of Labor Statistics, World Bank, IMF data sets
- **Earnings Calls**: Transcripts from Seeking Alpha, Motley Fool Transcripts

---

## Step 3 — Query Design Through Financial Lens
Each query must target specific, measurable financial data:

### Foundation Layer
- **Company/sector overview**: Revenue breakdown by segment, geographic distribution, business model, competitive positioning
- **Financial statements analysis**: Income statement trends, balance sheet health, cash flow generation, key ratios (P/E, P/B, ROE, ROIC, debt/equity)
- **Industry context**: Market size (TAM/SAM/SOM), growth rates, competitive landscape, regulatory environment

### Analytical Layer
- **Valuation analysis**: DCF, comparable company analysis, precedent transactions, sum-of-parts
- **Growth drivers and risks**: What drives top-line growth? What are the margin headwinds/tailwinds? What could go wrong?
- **Management assessment**: Track record, capital allocation history, insider transactions, compensation alignment

### Forward-Looking Layer
- **Analyst consensus**: Price targets, earnings estimates, rating distribution (buy/hold/sell)
- **Catalysts and events**: Upcoming earnings, product launches, regulatory decisions, macro events
- **Scenario analysis**: Bull case / base case / bear case with quantified outcomes

---

## Step 4 — Data Representation
- **Always use specific numbers**: Revenue in millions/billions with year, margins as percentages, growth rates YoY
- **Tables for financial data**: Quarterly/annual financials, ratio comparisons, peer benchmarks
- **Time-stamped data**: Always include the period (Q3 2025, FY2024, etc.)
- **Proper financial terminology**: EV/EBITDA not "company value ratio", CAGR not "average growth"
- **Currency denomination**: Always specify (USD, EUR, etc.)
- **Attribution**: Cite the specific report, filing, or data source

---

## Step 5 — Depth Calibration
| User Signal | Response |
|-------------|----------|
| "stock" / "invest" / "buy" | Full equity research: financials + valuation + catalysts + risks |
| "earnings" / "quarterly" | Detailed earnings analysis with consensus comparison |
| "market" / "economy" / "macro" | Macro overview with data from central banks and economic indicators |
| "compare" / "vs" | Side-by-side peer analysis with identical metrics |
| "personal finance" / "portfolio" | Strategy-oriented with asset allocation and risk-return profiles |
| Company name mentioned | Deep fundamental analysis of that specific company |

---

## Anti-Patterns
- ✗ Do NOT present opinions as facts — clearly separate analysis from data
- ✗ Do NOT use outdated financial data — always target the most recent filings/reports
- ✗ Do NOT ignore risk factors — every financial thesis has risks
- ✗ Do NOT conflate revenue with profit, market cap with enterprise value, or stock price with valuation
- ✗ Do NOT provide investment advice language ("you should buy") — present analysis frameworks
- ✗ Do NOT skip the "so what" — raw data without interpretation is useless in finance
