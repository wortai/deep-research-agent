Inspired by the success of Transformers in NLP, Vision Transformers (ViTs) divide an image into a set of tokens. This involves dividing the image into patches. These tokens are then encoded to produce an attention matrix that serves as a fundamental component of the self-attention mechanism.

**Q:** In what ways does a Vision Transformer's architecture resemble that of a standard Transformer used for text processing?

A ViT decomposes an input image into a series of patches (rather than text into tokens), serializes each patch into a vector, and maps it to a smaller dimension with a single matrix multiplication. These vector embeddings are then processed by a transformer encoder as if they were token embeddings. ViTs are based on the same attention mechanism as the original Transformer. However, ViTs typically use only the encoder part of the Transformer architecture, while standard Transformers for NLP often have both encoder and decoder components. The output of the encoder is passed to a neural network head for prediction. The transformer architecture in ViTs provides a versatile and modular foundation for building and adapting models.

**Q:** What architectural modifications are commonly applied to Vision Transformers (ViTs) compared to the original Transformer encoder from NLP?

Vision Transformers (ViTs) adapt the Transformer architecture, originally designed for NLP, for image classification tasks. A key modification is the use of only the encoder branch of the Transformer, whereas NLP transformers often utilize both encoder and decoder branches.
